The Eggplanter

Crispy Eggplant Fries

crunchy on the outside and tender on the inside

By Diana Rattray Updated 06/30/19

Description

Total
35 mins
Prep
20 mins
Cook
15 mins
Yield
4 to 6 servings

These deep-fried eggplant strips are crispy and delicious, and they are an excellent alternative to higher carb French fries. Coated in flour, then dipped in an egg wash, and rolled in breadcrumbs, the eggplant strips fry up to be crunchy on the outside and tender on the inside. The coating is not as thick as a battered deep-fried eggplant strip, but this version is melt-in-your-mouth goodness none the less.

These eggplant strips are wonderful on their own, but when served with a flavored mayonnaise, tomato or tomatillo salsa, or a ranch or blue cheese dip, they become an impressive appetizer or even afternoon snackâ€”and your family and guests will be begging for more. You can also sprinkle the fried eggplant strips with Parmesan cheese and serve them with warm marinara sauce as a nod to an eggplant Parm.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium eggplant
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • 3/4 cup dried breadcrumbs
  • Pinch of kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Optional: Lemon halves for serving

Steps to Make It

  1. Peel the eggplant using a sharp knife or vegetable peeler. Slice it into 1/4-inch-thick sticks about 3 inches in length.
  2. Put about 2 inches of oil in a deep, heavy saucepan and heat to 370 F (188 C).
  3. Put the flour in a shallow bowl. In another bowl, beat the egg with the water. Put the breadcrumbs in a third bowl.
  4. Sprinkle the eggplant pieces lightly with salt and freshly ground black pepper.
  5. Roll the eggplant pieces in the flour, shake off any excess, and then dip into egg mixture. Let excess egg drip off before rolling the strips in the breadcrumbs to coat.
  6. Fry the coated eggplant pieces in the hot oil until golden brown, about 2 to 3 minutes, turning them as they cook.
  7. Transfer the eggplant strips to paper towels to drain and sprinkle lightly with kosher salt. Serve hot with lemon halves or a dip, if desired.
